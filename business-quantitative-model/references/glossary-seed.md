# Methodology Glossary — Seed

This is the canonical seed the `business-quantitative-model` skill copies to `docs/business/models/glossary.md` on first use in a project. **Trim it to the terms actually used in the project's model set** — do not paste the full seed if many terms are unused.

Two sections only:

1. **Metrics & acronyms** — standard financial and SaaS/growth metrics used in model inputs, outputs, and scenario matrices
2. **Pricing methodology** — terms from value-based pricing literature used in headroom analysis and scenario interpretation

Domain jargon (e.g., OR, IOL, FTE, TARMED) and project-internal IDs (e.g., C2.2, P-01) belong elsewhere — in the project's domain glossary or in the model's §1 notes.

## Metrics & acronyms

**ACV** — Annual Contract Value. The annualised revenue of a subscription contract, normalised to one year regardless of contract length. Distinct from ARR when multi-year contracts have variable payment schedules.

**ARR** — Annual Recurring Revenue. The annualised value of all active subscription contracts at a point in time. Primary SaaS growth metric. `ARR = MRR × 12`.

**B2B** — Business to Business. A commercial relationship where the customer is an organisation, not an individual consumer.

**BoM** — Bill of Materials. Itemised list of components and their unit costs required to produce one unit of a physical product.

**CAC** — Customer Acquisition Cost. Total sales and marketing spend divided by the number of new customers acquired in the same period. Vendor-side metric — belongs in the unit-economics model (3a), not the customer ROI model (1a).

**CAC payback period (vendor-side)** — Time for the vendor to recoup CAC from gross margin generated by the customer. Formula: `CAC ÷ (MRR × gross margin %)`. Healthy benchmarks: <12 months (SMB) · <18 months (mid-market) · <24 months (enterprise), per Bessemer State of Cloud 2025. Distinct from customer-side payback — see *Payback period* under Pricing methodology.

**COGS** — Cost of Goods Sold. Direct costs attributable to producing a product or delivering a service; excludes operating overhead.

**GMV** — Gross Merchandise Value. Total transaction value processed through a marketplace or platform, before the platform's take-rate is deducted. A volume metric, not a revenue metric.

**GRR** — Gross Revenue Retention. Percentage of recurring revenue retained from existing customers, excluding upsells. Measures churn only. Formula: `(starting ARR − churned ARR − contraction ARR) ÷ starting ARR`.

**LTV** — Lifetime Value (also CLV — Customer Lifetime Value). Total revenue (or gross profit) a customer generates over their entire relationship with the vendor. Simple formula: `ACV ÷ annual churn rate`.

**LTV:CAC ratio** — Ratio of customer lifetime value to customer acquisition cost. Minimum viable: 3:1. Healthy: 5:1. Below 1:1 means the business destroys value acquiring customers.

**MRR** — Monthly Recurring Revenue. Monthly equivalent of ARR. `ARR = MRR × 12`.

**MSRP** — Manufacturer's Suggested Retail Price. The vendor's recommended end-customer price for a physical product, before channel discounts.

**NRR** — Net Revenue Retention. Percentage of recurring revenue retained from existing customers *including* upsells and expansions. NRR > 100% means the existing customer base grows without new logos.

**ROI** — Return on Investment. In model context: annual currency value the product creates for the customer. Formula (general): `(gain − cost) ÷ cost`. In the 1a customer ROI model, "ROI" is the raw CHF/yr gain — the division by cost happens in the 1b pricing headroom model.

**SAM** — Serviceable Addressable Market. The subset of TAM reachable by the company's current product and go-to-market motion within a defined time horizon.

**SaaS** — Software as a Service. Software hosted by the vendor and sold as a subscription. Distinguishing characteristic: recurring revenue, near-zero marginal cost to serve, platform economics.

**SMB** — Small and Medium Business. Commonly: <500 employees and/or <€50M revenue, though classification thresholds vary by authority.

**SOM** — Serviceable Obtainable Market. The subset of SAM the company can realistically capture in the near term (Year 1–2) given sales capacity and competitive dynamics. The pitch-relevant number.

**TAM** — Total Addressable Market. The total revenue opportunity if the product captured 100% of the relevant market at the proposed price. A ceiling, not a target. Must be paired with SAM and SOM or it is meaningless as a pitch figure.

**WTP** — Willingness to Pay. The maximum price a customer will pay before choosing an alternative or walking away. Validated by pricing conversations and signed contracts — not assumed from list prices or internal workshopping.

## Pricing methodology

**Calibration (§6 calibration callout)** — Comparison of the model's Base-case headline figure against an external expectation (investor norm, peer benchmark, or industry comparable). Forces honest acknowledgement of whether the pitch math clears the bar before the deck is shown to anyone.

**Confidence stars** — 5-star scale anchoring each model input to its evidence type. ★★★★★ primary government/regulator or verified internal data · ★★★★ single-source authoritative industry data · ★★★ triangulated multi-source industry data · ★★ internal hypothesis or workshop output (directional only) · ★ anecdote or single weak-source signal (must be flagged in §8 Key Unknowns).

**Conservative / Base / Aggressive scenarios** — The three columns of every scenario matrix. Conservative = pessimistic assumption on the model's softest input; Aggressive = optimistic; Base = the most defensible central estimate. A model never reports a single point estimate — that pretends away the uncertainty.

**Funnel** — The sequential reduction of a population or quantity through model steps: top of funnel → filter → conversion → segmentation. Each step applies a rate or filter to the previous step's output. The shape (3-step, 4-step, 5-step) is chosen to match the venture type.

**Headroom** — The ratio of customer ROI to subscription price (`ROI ÷ price`). A headroom of 5× means the customer receives five times more value than they pay annually. Determines how much upward pricing flex exists before customer ROI turns unattractive.

**Implicit assumptions table (§5.2)** — The mandatory table listing every structural shortcut the model takes that could be wrong. Each row carries a risk rating: ✅ probably right with stated reason · ⚠️ risky oversimplification with specific failure mode and validation path · ❌ known wrong, kept as a floor or ceiling pending better data. The quality differentiator that separates a defensible model from a confident-looking spreadsheet.

**Payback period (customer-side)** — Time for the customer to recoup the subscription price from realised value. Formula: `(annual price ÷ annual ROI) × 12 months`. Distinct from vendor-side CAC payback. Healthy target: <12 months for any B2B SaaS purchase a department head can approve without a capital committee.

**Penetration pricing band** — Value-capture ratio of 10–20%. Entry-market positioning; lowers adoption friction and accelerates reference-customer acquisition. Per [Orb Billing three-band framework](https://www.withorb.com/blog/value-based-pricing-formula).

**Standard pricing band** — Value-capture ratio of 20–40%. The defensible centre of B2B SaaS literature. Both vendor and customer retain meaningful upside from the deal. Per [Orb Billing three-band framework](https://www.withorb.com/blog/value-based-pricing-formula).

**Strong differentiation band** — Value-capture ratio of 40–50%. Defensible when the vendor holds a first-mover or monopoly position and WTP has been validated by real contracts. Per [Orb Billing three-band framework](https://www.withorb.com/blog/value-based-pricing-formula). Note: [LeveragePoint](https://www.leveragepoint.com/blog/pricing/what-percent-of-value-should-we-capture-in-price/) cautions against treating any of these percentages as universal laws — context and competitive position matter more than the band.

**Top-of-funnel** — The total relevant universe before any filtering. The §2 anchor of every model. Should be the narrowest defensible claim for the specific opportunity, not the broadest possible universe. Common error: using a global or national aggregate when the product only touches a defined subset.

**Value-based pricing** — Setting price as a fraction of the value the product delivers to the customer, rather than as a cost-plus markup. Requires a credible customer ROI model (1a) as its input. The dominant pricing methodology for B2B SaaS.

**Value-capture ratio** — The fraction of customer annual ROI captured by the vendor's annual price. Formula: `annual price ÷ annual customer ROI`. The central metric of a pricing headroom model (1b). Interpretive bands: <10% under-priced · 10–20% penetration · 20–40% standard · 40–50% strong differentiation · >50% risks WTP pushback.
