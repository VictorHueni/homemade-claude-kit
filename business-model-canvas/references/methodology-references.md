# Business Model Canvas — Methodology References

This document records the canonical frameworks used to design and
maintain Business Model Canvas (BMC), Lean Canvas, and Value Proposition
Canvas (VPC) artefacts. The artefact is a synthesis of four primary
sources plus practitioner discipline — each contributes a specific lens.

The most important methodological choice up front is the **variant**:
BMC (Osterwalder/Pigneur) for established / strategic-documentation
contexts, or Lean Canvas (Maurya) for startup / problem-discovery
contexts. They share 5 of 9 blocks; Lean Canvas substitutes the other 4.

---

## 1. Osterwalder & Pigneur — *Business Model Generation* (2010)

**Used for:** the canonical 9-block Business Model Canvas, the left/right
(infrastructure/customer) framing, the value-proposition-as-keystone
principle, and the "canvas is a conversation tool" discipline.

**Source:** Alexander Osterwalder & Yves Pigneur, *Business Model
Generation: A Handbook for Visionaries, Game Changers, and Challengers*
(Wiley, 2010). Canonical online summary:
[Strategyzer — Business Model Generation Book Summary](https://www.strategyzer.com/library/business-model-generation-book-summary).
Building-block reference:
[Strategyzer — Business Model Canvas Building Blocks](https://www.strategyzer.com/business-model-canvas/building-blocks).

### Key contributions

- **Definition:** *"A business model is nothing other than a
  representation of how an organization makes (or intends to make)
  money."*
- **9 blocks:** Customer Segments · Value Propositions · Channels ·
  Customer Relationships · Revenue Streams · Key Resources · Key
  Activities · Key Partnerships · Cost Structure.
- **Layout principle:** right side = customer-facing (who you serve and
  how you earn); left side = infrastructure (what you need to make it
  work); centre = Value Propositions (the keystone).
- **Iterative testing:** *"test your business model assumptions early
  and often"* — the canvas is a diagnostic + creation tool, not a static
  artefact.
- **Customer-first principle:** *"Empathize with your customers to
  discover what they really need"* before designing the rest of the
  model.

### Discipline encoded in the template

- The BMC variant maps 1:1 to Osterwalder's 9 blocks.
- The Value Propositions block sits centrally; soft-links to VPC
  drill-downs and value-stream IDs preserve the "VP-as-keystone"
  principle.
- The skill's changelog requirement reflects the "conversation tool, not
  static document" discipline.

## 2. Osterwalder — *Value Proposition Design* (2014)

**Used for:** the Value Proposition Canvas (VPC) — the per-segment
drill-down into the Value Propositions block of the BMC. Customer
Profile (Jobs/Pains/Gains) × Value Map (Products/Pain
Relievers/Gain Creators) = product-market fit.

**Source:** Alexander Osterwalder, Yves Pigneur, Gregory Bernarda &
Alan Smith, *Value Proposition Design: How to Create Products and
Services Customers Want* (Wiley, 2014). Canonical online tool:
[Strategyzer — Value Proposition Canvas](https://www.strategyzer.com/library/the-value-proposition-canvas).
Book summary:
[Strategyzer — Value Proposition Design Book Summary](https://www.strategyzer.com/library/value-proposition-design-book-summary).

### Key contributions

- **VPC = drill into one BMC block.** *"a detailed tool for modeling
  the relationship between two parts of the Osterwalder's broader
  Business Model Canvas; customer segments and value propositions."*
- **Customer Profile (3 components):**
  - **Jobs-to-be-done:** *"Identify your customer's major
    Jobs-to-be-done"* — functional, emotional, social jobs.
  - **Pains:** *"the pains they face when trying to accomplish their
    Jobs-to-be-done"*.
  - **Gains:** *"the gains they perceive by getting their jobs done"*.
- **Value Map (3 components):**
  - **Products & Services:** *"the most important components of your
    offering"*.
  - **Pain Relievers:** how the offering relieves customer pain.
  - **Gain Creators:** how the offering creates customer gains.
- **Achieving fit:** product-market fit is achieved when *"the
  products and services offered as part of the value proposition
  address the most significant pains and gains from the customer
  profile."*

### Discipline encoded in the template

- VPC is an optional companion mode (one VPC file per Tier-1 segment).
- The VPC template includes a "Fit check" table that explicitly maps
  each pain to a reliever and each gain to a creator — surfaces
  unaddressed pains (product-market-fit gaps) and unused relievers
  (feature bloat).
- The skill enforces ranking pains by intensity and gains by importance
  per Osterwalder's guidance.

## 3. Ash Maurya — *Running Lean* (2010) / Lean Canvas

**Used for:** the Lean Canvas variant — replaces 4 of the BMC blocks to
shift focus from documentation-of-stable-model to discovery-of-uncertain-
model. The startup-context variant.

**Source:** Ash Maurya, *Running Lean: Iterate from Plan A to a Plan That
Works* (3rd ed., O'Reilly 2022). Founder's introduction:
[Why Lean Canvas vs Business Model Canvas?](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/why-lean-canvas-vs-business-model-ash-maurya).

### Key contributions

- **Purpose:** *"The Lean Canvas is heavily problem focused"* —
  designed for startups, new product ideas, and high-uncertainty
  environments.
- **4 block substitutions vs BMC:**
  - Problem (replaces Key Partners)
  - Solution (replaces Key Activities)
  - Key Metrics (replaces Key Resources)
  - Unfair Advantage (replaces Customer Relationships)
- **Shared blocks (5 of 9):** Customer Segments, Unique Value
  Proposition (note: BMC calls it Value Propositions), Channels,
  Revenue Streams, Cost Structure.
- **Discipline:** test the riskiest parts of the model first; pivot the
  Problem before pivoting the Solution.

### Discipline encoded in the template

- The variant choice is binary at scaffold; no mixing within one
  canvas.
- The Lean Canvas template requires 1–3 specific problems with evidence
  (not generic statements like "slow processes") + Existing
  alternatives — pushing the user to surface what customers do *today*.
- The skill suggests Lean Canvas for early-stage projects and BMC for
  established / scaling contexts but always lets the user pick.

## 4. Strategyzer — Practitioner Discipline

**Used for:** the "canvas is a conversation tool" principle, the
sticky-note brevity discipline, the inter-block dependency rule, the
confidence-rating practice.

**Source:** Strategyzer (Alexander Osterwalder's company) practitioner
guidance and tools:
- [Strategyzer — What is a Business Model?](https://www.strategyzer.com/library/what-is-a-business-model)
- [Strategyzer — Business Model Canvas Building Blocks](https://www.strategyzer.com/business-model-canvas/building-blocks)

### Key contributions

- **Sticky-note brevity:** each block carries 3–7 bullets max; if a
  block needs paragraphs, the depth belongs in a linked companion doc.
- **Inter-block dependencies:** *"each block is connected, and a
  decision in one box will ripple through the others… think of the
  canvas as a system, not a set of silos."*
- **Confidence rating:** Strategyzer's practitioner literature
  emphasises labelling each block as `Assumed` / `Tested` / `Validated`
  to surface what hasn't been proven yet — the canvas serves the
  testing loop, not just the documentation loop.
- **Conversation tool:** *"the canvas is a conversation tool"* — a
  solo draft is a starting point; team review and challenge make it
  real.

### Discipline encoded in the template

- 3–7 bullets per block; depth pushed to companion docs.
- Confidence rating per block (default `Assumed`).
- Changelog mandatory for every refresh.
- Inter-block coherence check at the bottom of the canvas: trace one
  segment through every block to verify the model holds together.

## 5. Practitioner anti-pattern literature

**Used for:** the 8 anti-patterns the skill guards against. Lifted from
multiple practitioner sources (LinkedIn essays, Medium guides,
Strategyzer-aligned consultancies).

**Sources:**
- [Khuram Zaman — The Business Model Canvas: Five Common Mistakes](https://zamandigital.medium.com/the-business-model-canvas-five-common-mistakes-d4b622783cde)
- [Isaac Jeffries — Nine Big Business Model Canvas Mistakes](https://isaacjeffries.com/blog/2016/2/3/nine-big-business-model-canvas-mistakes)
- [TinkTide — 10 Pitfalls to Dodge When Using the BMC](https://tinktide.com/resources/10-common-mistakes-to-avoid-when-using-business-model-canvas)
- [RIKON — Common Mistakes in Building a BMC](https://www.rikon.ie/common-mistakes-building-business-model-canvas/)

### Key contributions

The 8 anti-patterns encoded in the skill:
1. **Vagueness** — generic nouns instead of specific segments / value statements.
2. **VP-as-features** — listing features instead of value delivered.
3. **Org-units as segments** — "Marketing" is a department, not a segment.
4. **Static document** — no changelog, no refresh, no testing loop.
5. **Inter-block silos** — ignoring how a change in one block cascades.
6. **Skipping assumption-testing** — every `Assumed` bullet is a hypothesis.
7. **Solo draft as final** — the canvas is a conversation tool.
8. **Current/future state mixing** — one canvas per timeframe.

## 6. (Related but out of scope) — Mission Model Canvas, Lean Stack, Job-to-be-Done Canvas

**Used for:** awareness only. The skill does not produce these variants
in v1. If the user explicitly asks for one of them, redirect or note as
out-of-scope.

**Sources:**
- **Mission Model Canvas** (Steve Blank, 2016) — government / nonprofit
  variant of the BMC. Replaces Revenue Streams with Mission
  Achievement, Cost Structure with Mission Cost / Budget, Customer
  Segments with Beneficiaries.
- **Lean Stack** (Ash Maurya extension) — a fuller decision-support
  toolkit around the Lean Canvas with traction models, customer-factory
  models, etc.
- **JTBD Canvas** — Tony Ulwick's outcome-driven innovation tool;
  drills into Jobs-to-be-Done in a different way from the VPC.

These are not produced by this skill in v1. They can be added as new
variants in future versions.

## Summary — what each framework contributes

| Framework | Contributes |
|---|---|
| Osterwalder & Pigneur — Business Model Generation | The canonical 9-block BMC; layout principle; VP-as-keystone; iterative testing |
| Osterwalder — Value Proposition Design | VPC structure (Customer Profile × Value Map); fit-check methodology |
| Maurya — Lean Canvas | Startup variant; 4 block substitutions; problem-first discipline |
| Strategyzer practitioner | Sticky-note brevity; inter-block dependency; conversation tool; confidence rating |
| Practitioner anti-pattern literature | 8 common mistakes catalogue |

The template is not "Osterwalder BMC" or "Maurya Lean Canvas" alone — it
supports both variants and adds the VPC companion. Every section maps
back to at least one source. The variant choice (BMC vs Lean) is the
biggest methodological decision; the confidence rating + changelog are
the discipline that keeps the canvas alive as a testing tool, not a
poster.
