## Iteration 5
- Increment: 05 — Create the `util-open-items` Skill
- Status: done
- Files changed: util-open-items/SKILL.md (new), util-open-items/references/template.md (new), util-open-items/references/triage-rules.md (new), docs/exec-plans/active/0001_open-items-governance/0001_exec_open-items-governance.md
- Test gate: passed (gate 1: SKILL.md exists; gate 2: all 9 required tokens present — sync × 38, triage × 24, close × 28, report × 25, OI-ID × 8, Source anchor × 20, Source heading × 17, `## Open Items` × 24, project-control/open-items/open-items.md × 9)
- Learnings: util-open-items is the only sanctioned writer of `project-control/open-items/open-items.md` — every other skill in the kit emits open items into its own artefact's document-level `## Open Items` section and then chains to this skill (chaining language lands in Increment 06). Six modes were defined: sync (roll local rows into the ledger + mint canonical `OI-NNNN` IDs starting at OI-0001), triage (propose owner/priority/de-duplication changes — never mutates silently), close (terminal state with mandatory non-_TBD_ Tracker ref), drop (terminal state with explicit rationale in Resolution path), archive (move terminal rows older than 30 days into `archive/<YYYY-Q[1-4]>.md`), report (governance-health snapshot to `var/reports/open-items/`). De-duplication policy uses three coordinates (Source artefact + Source anchor OR Source heading + ≥80% Summary overlap OR shared Resolution path) — this is stricter than path-only matching because the central plane has to survive both heading edits and anchor renames without losing identity. Closure semantics are event-driven, not date-driven: reaching a Due / Review date without a Tracker ref does NOT auto-close; it surfaces in triage as an escalation candidate — this preserves the contract that closure requires evidence. The 30-day linger period between close and archive is intentional: it is the difference between "we resolved this" and "we have forgotten about it", and gives retrospectives a window to scan recent resolutions for patterns. Two reference files split methodology from operations: `template.md` carries the canonical 11-column local schema, the 12-column ledger schema with `Source artefact` inserted after `Summary`, and three worked end-to-end examples (sync, close, archive); `triage-rules.md` carries the four signals (de-duplication, ownerless rows, stalled high-priority rows, blocked rows without exit criteria) with finding levels (info/warn/escalate) and proposal templates. SKILL.md sets `impact: medium` (mutates ledger + writes back into source artefacts), `allow_implicit_invocation: true` (chained from other skills), `last_reviewed: 2026-05-25`, `review_interval: 180d` (utility skill). install.sh re-ran cleanly (3 changed, 0 pruned) and the symlink at ~/.claude/skills/util-open-items resolves into the kit. Milestone M3 (Ledger Automation, increments 05-06) moves from pending to in-progress; increment 06 adds the chaining language to the seven artefact-producing skills already named in the plan.
