## Facilitation tips

### Before you start

- **Set expectations**: *"This is not a presentation. We're building together. Every idea is valid."*
- **Ground rules** (write on whiteboard and keep visible):
  - One conversation at a time
  - Build on ideas ("Yes, and..." not "Yes, but...")
  - Decisions by dot-voting, not debate
  - Phones face down
  - Every voice matters equally — no hierarchy here

### Energy management

- Morning sessions (9:00–13:00) work best for first-timers
- Alternate silent individual work ↔ group discussion every 15-20 min
- Stand-up exercises beat sitting exercises
- Break every 60-75 min

### Engagement techniques

- **Silent writing before discussion** — Prevents the loudest voice from dominating. Always have people write post-its individually BEFORE sharing aloud
- **Dot-voting over debate** — When opinions diverge, vote with stickers. Democratic, fast, no hard feelings
- **Timeboxing** — Visible timer. *"Vous avez 5 minutes"* creates focus
- **Persona champions** — Each participant "adopts" a persona — they represent that user's interests in all discussions
- **Parking lot** — Dedicated wall space for off-topic ideas. Promise to revisit. Prevents derailing

### Handling disagreements (Kaner)

- *"On note les deux options et on vote"* — not *"on débat jusqu'à ce que quelqu'un cède"*
- **Disagree and Commit**: Vote, minority commits to majority decision. Revisit in 4 weeks with data
- For fundamental strategic disagreements: park them, schedule a dedicated 1h follow-up session
- Use **Wicked Questions** (Liberating Structure) to surface paradoxes when stuck
