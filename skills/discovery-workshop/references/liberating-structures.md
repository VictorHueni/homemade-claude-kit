# Liberating Structures — Internal Claude Guidance

This file is **not** copied into the project. It catalogues the 33
Liberating Structures (Lipmanowicz & McCandless, 2014) with when-to-use
guidance. Consult during workshop-agenda generation to pick the right
microstructure per exercise.

> Canonical reference: [liberatingstructures.com](https://www.liberatingstructures.com/).
> Quick-pick guide: [SessionLab — Liberating Structures](https://www.sessionlab.com/library/liberating-structures).

---

## The 5 elements of every Liberating Structure

Every microstructure specifies five elements precisely:

1. **Structuring invitation** — the prompt or question
2. **How space is arranged** — physical setup
3. **How participation is distributed** — who speaks when
4. **How groups are configured** — pairs, fours, all
5. **Sequence of steps and time allocation** — minute-by-minute flow

When writing an exercise in the agenda, name the LS and let the canonical
spec define the structure. Don't reinvent.

| LS | Best for | Time | Group size |

| Sequence | Components | Best for |

## Quick-pick guide by workshop type

### BMC workshop (Strategyzer canvas)

1. **Impromptu Networking** (warm-up, 20 min)
2. Per block: **1-2-4-All** on the block's structuring question (15 min × 9 blocks = 2h)
3. **Min Specs** to identify the most-uncertain bullets (30 min)
4. **15% Solutions** — each participant names one validation action they'll take in 7 days (15 min)

### Persona workshop

1. **Impromptu Networking** (20 min)
2. **Drawing Together** — sketch the persona as a group (40 min)
3. **9 Whys** — drill into the persona's primary frustration (20 min)
4. **Min Specs** for what makes this persona distinct (30 min)
5. **What-So-What-Now-What** closure (15 min)

### Value Stream workshop

1. **Heard, Seen, Respected** (warm-up, 25 min)
2. **Drawing Together** — sketch the stream stages (40 min)
3. **Wicked Questions** — surface tensions (e.g., quality vs speed) at each stage (20 min)
4. **TRIZ** — what's the worst version of this stream? (30 min)
5. **15% Solutions** for improving the most painful stage (15 min)

### Capability Map workshop

1. **Impromptu Networking** (20 min)
2. **Min Specs** — minimum capabilities the business must have (45 min)
3. **25/10 Crowd Sourcing** — rank capabilities by Differentiator / Necessary / Commodity (30 min)
4. **Wicked Questions** — paradoxes (e.g., "build vs buy"; "specialist vs generalist") (20 min)
5. **What-So-What-Now-What** closure (15 min)

### Discovery / Design Sprint kickoff

1. **Impromptu Networking** (20 min)
2. **9 Whys** — drill into the sprint question (25 min)
3. **Conversation Café** — open exploration of the problem space (60 min)
4. **Wicked Questions** — paradoxes to navigate (20 min)
5. **Critical Uncertainties** — scenarios that could derail us (60 min)
6. **15% Solutions** — what each person owns next (15 min)

## When NOT to use Liberating Structures

- **One-way information transfer.** Just present. Don't ceremoniously
  call it a workshop.
- **Tight deadline meetings.** A 20-min status sync doesn't need
  microstructures.
- **Single-decision conversations.** "Should we approve budget X?" —
  just decide; don't workshop it.
- **Confidential 1:1 feedback.** Use a private conversation, not group
  microstructures.

If the workshop has ≤2 participants, drop Liberating Structures and use
the `discovery-research` 1:1 interview skill instead.

## Disagreement-handling structures (when Diamond's Groan zone stalls)

When the group is stuck in the messy middle (Kaner Diamond's Groan zone),
deploy:

1. **Wicked Questions** — name the paradox; legitimise it; don't try to "solve" it
2. **TRIZ** — "make it worse" — surfaces counterproductive habits
3. **Helping Heuristics** — pair-up; partner helps you see your own blind spot
4. **Fishbowl** — let two people deeply disagree publicly; everyone learns

Don't push through Groan zone with dot-voting; the unresolved tension
will resurface.

## Quality checks before naming an exercise

For every exercise in the agenda:

- [ ] Named structure (LS or other canonical method) — NOT "brainstorm" / "discussion"
- [ ] Timebox specified (5-60 min typical)
- [ ] Output specified (what exists at the end of this exercise)
- [ ] Diamond stage assigned (Divergent / Groan / Convergent / Closure)
- [ ] Materials listed (post-its, markers, dots, board, etc.)
